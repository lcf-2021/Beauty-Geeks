How to be Confidently Beautiful

Women slowly become self-conscious with their looks once they check their birthdays again. They take one look at the year, then another look at the mirror. Their self-esteem plummets as fast as their smile. They worry the bland eyelids, simple eyes, chapped lips, and acne dotted cheeks would tarnish the reputation of how people look at them, and that isn’t the right mindset for a flower still waiting to bloom. Girls of younger ages are especially the most notorious for being nit-picky about their still growing skin, how said skin would react to acne is still up to how they grow up, but they worry nonetheless about how to hide it or how to embrace it. Girls of 20s or so take a bit of a while until they realize they needed to retain that youthful flourish to feel young, to feel fresh, and to feel like they have a chance. It’s a raw way of thinking in the coming generations, so raw that it may feel forced. Forced to feel beautiful. This article tackles that rawness in its fullest form, showing what beauty really should mean to the eyes of the youthful and blossomed.

To be beautiful in others’ eyes, you must first be beautiful to your own. An easy step towards the surface of what this truly means focuses towards your features. Always think in a way that is half-full and rarely if never half-empty. Love your features rather than hide them behind prosthetics. Instead of hiding behind make-up, glorify your already amazing features by accentuating them with products of make-up. Your face, your outfit and your body shape shouldn’t stop you from feeling beautiful every second of every day of your life. Being beautiful is subjective. You’d see yourself on the end of your critique and would never be able to pull yourself out of your mindset. You’d never see yourself as something more. That’s why. you should go about this step by step. Wear the things you love with pride, touch up a bit but enough to make you feel comfortable. Find a pair of things you love that would go great on you. Do not fish out compliments, it’ll give you a source of ranking to how you should dress. Instead, don’t stick to one thing every day. To be confidently beautiful, you shouldn’t be afraid of change. Be confident in what you want, and that want is change. Change is beautiful in both the eyes of people who adore passion and to your own eyes as well.

Being beautiful isn’t a trudge of a task. It only means “How do I want to see myself?”. Accept the fact that you are beautiful, you were and always will be as long as you keep that positive and bold personality. It’s a hard front to keep up at first, but being beautiful is about loving what you already have on you and being passionate about it. It could also be seen as vaguely sexy to many, but to all, it is a beautiful and confident look that you yourself can adore with just one mindset. Bold. Passionate. Beautiful.
