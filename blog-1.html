Know Your Best Friends: A Step by Step Make-up Guide

1. Primer

Priming is the first step in doing your make-up. A primer serves as a base or layer between your skin and make-up. It smoothens and evens out the skin. It also helps the make-up stay longer.

Recommended products:

NYX Pore Filler Primer
High-end

The NYX Pore Filler Primer has a light, peachy tint to it. If you’re someone with a medium to dark skin tone, you might not like the effect it leaves on your skin. Its formula is lightweight and easy to apply, and it is the best primer for pores. It also helps control oil and keeps shine at bay.

P840.00

Maybelline Baby Skin Instant Pore Eraser
Drugstore

The Maybelline Baby Skin Instant Pore Eraser is one of the most popular drugstore primers, and we get why it’s such a great product. From its price to its performance – everything is exceptional. The primer comes in a small, stylish blue tube and Maybelline wasn’t kidding when they named it “Baby Skin” for it completely transforms the look and feel of your skin by making it incredibly soft.

The translucent gel primer is great for anyone looking to create a matte and flawless canvas – it fills in pores, hydrates your skin, and increases the longevity of your makeup for up to 6-7 hours. Mature skin that needs some extra hydration will love this stuff!

P249.00

2. Liquid foundation

Liquid foundation can be applied with a brush or sponge. With the right shade, amount and blending, it can perfect your skin by covering flaws and evening out and correcting your color complexion. Make sure to pick the shade that matches your skin. We don’t want you looking like clowns.

3. Concealer

It’s best to apply concealer after finishing your foundation. The two products are similar but a concealer has higher coverage. It masks dark circles, pimples, blemishes and pores. It’s the key to flawlessnes.

Double Duty means you can use this product as both a concealer and a contour/highlight. It’s available in 14 shades, and the lightest shade is very light and perfect for highlighting pale to light skin.

4. Setting powder

A setting powder holds or sets your foundation and concealer. It also reduces the sticky feeling of applied foundation on your face. A setting powder mattifies your face so make sure you don’t add more foundation and concealer on the layer above powder or else your make-up will look cakey.

7. Brows

Brow products are used to define and fill in the eyebrows. You can use a pencil, pomade, powder, or gel, whatever you’re comfortable with. A great advice to remember when shaping your brows is that they’re not twins, they’re sisters. Therefore, your brows don’t have to be perfectly symmetrical, just follow the natural shape, give them a little color and you’re good to go.

12. Highlighter
